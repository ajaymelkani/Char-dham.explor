Nainital is a city and an important tourist destination located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand state of India . It is also the headquarters of the district. [ 1 ] Nainital has special significance in the Kumaon region. It comes in 'Chhatta' pargana. The name 'Chhatta' is derived from 'Shashtikhat'. 'Shashtikhat' means sixty lakes. Earlier there were sixty picturesque lakes in this region. That is why this area was called 'Shashtikhat'. Today this region is more known by the name 'Chhatta'. It is called the Lake District of India, because this entire place is surrounded by lakes . The word 'Naini' means eyes and 'Tal' means lake. Situated between snow-covered mountains, this place is surrounded by lakes. The most prominent lake among these is Naini Lake, after which this place is named Nainital. [ 2 ] [ 3 ] Nainital Length – 1500 meters, Width – 510 meters, Depth – 10 to 156 meters
Nainital is situated in the foothills of the Kumaon hills of the Himalayas. The total height of Nainital from sea level is about 1938 meters (6358 feet). There is a pear-shaped lake in the valley of Nainital which is known as Naini Lake . This lake is surrounded by mountains from all sides and its total circumference is about two miles. The highest peak of the mountains surrounding this lake is Naina Peak in the north , whose height is (2615 meters (8579 feet)), while Devpatha (2438 meters (7999 feet)) is situated to the west and Ayar Patha (2278 meters (7474 feet)) to the south. Panoramic views of the entire area are visible from these peaks.

Rishikesh

Hrishikesh ( Tadbhav : Rishikesh) is a town near Dehradun in Dehradun district of the Indian state of Uttarakhand . It is situated on the right bank of the Ganges River and is a pilgrimage site for Hindus, where ancient saints used to meditate here in search of higher knowledge. [ 1 ] [ 2 ] Many temples and ashrams are built on the banks of the river. [ 3 ] It is known as the " Gateway to the Garhwal Himalaya " and the "Yoga City of the World". [ 4 ] The town has hosted the annual "International Yoga Festival" in the first week of March since 1999. [ 5 ] [ 6 ] Hrishikesh is a vegetarian and alcohol-free town. [ 7 ]
The 3,000 year old Vasishtha cave is located on the Badrinath-Kedarnath road at a distance of 22 km from Rishikesh. Many sages can be seen resting and meditating at this place. It is said that this place was the residence of Vasishtha, the priest of Lord Rama and many kings. Sadhus can be seen in a meditative posture in the Vasishtha cave. A Shivalinga is also installed inside the cave. This place is very famous for tourism. Gun Hill Mussoorie

Gun Hill is one of the highest peaks in Mussoorie and can be easily reached by cable car from Mall Road. Situated at an altitude of over 2024 meters, Gun Hill offers a perfect opportunity to spend some quiet time admiring the Doon Valley and the mesmerizing snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas. It is about 400 feet above the Mall Road and will take less than 5 minutes to reach via ropeway. Adventure lovers can also walk to Gun Hill, but it involves a steep climb. Gun Hill used to be a place from where, during the British rule, a cannon was fired every day at noon so that people could set their watches. This popular tourist spot is a perfect place to watch the sun set behind the magnificent Himalayan mountains.
