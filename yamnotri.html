YAMNOTRI

Yamunotri is a part of Char Dham (along with Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath), the four most revered Hindu pilgrimages in the Himalayas. The small mountain hamlet, with the Yamunotri Temple at its centre, attracts thousands of devotees every year and is the commencing point of the Char Dham Yatra pilgrimage (May to October), which proceeds from Yamunotri to Gangotri and finally to Kedarnath and Badrinath. Gangotri

Gangaji Temple of Gangotri is situated at an altitude of 3042 meters above sea level. The environment on the right side of Bhagirathi is very attractive and enchanting. This place is situated at a distance of 100 km from Uttarkashi . Ganga Maiyya's temple was built by Gorkha commander Amar Singh Thapa in the beginning of the 18th century. The present temple was rebuilt by the royal family of Jaipur. Every year between the months of May to October, lakhs of devotees and pilgrims come here to see the purifying Ganga Maiyya. Like Yamunotri, the purifying temple of Gangotri also opens on the holy festival of Akshaya Tritiya and the doors of the temple are closed on the day of Diwali .
The history of Gangotri town and the temple is inseparably linked. In ancient times, there was no temple here. In Gangotri, the personified form of Mother Ganga, i.e. Ganga Dhara, was worshipped by Semwal priests. There was a platform near Bhagirathi rock where idols of gods and goddesses were kept for three to four months during the pilgrimage season. These idols were brought from villages like Mukhaba etc., which were returned to the same villages after the pilgrimage season.

BADARINATH

Badrinath or Badrinarayan Temple is a Hindu temple located on the banks of the Alaknanda River in Chamoli district of the Indian state of Uttarakhand . It is a temple dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu and is one of the four most sacred places described in this religion, the four Dhams . It is an ancient temple whose construction is evidenced to have been in the 7th-9th century. The town built around the temple is also called Badrinath after the name of the temple . Geographically, this place is located in the Garhwal region , at an altitude of 3,133 meters (10,279 ft) above sea level, among the high peaks of the Himalayan mountain range . Due to the harsh weather conditions of the Himalayan region during the winter season, the temple remains open for a limited period of six months of the year (from the end of April to the beginning of November). It is one of the busiest pilgrimage sites in India; In 2012, around 10.6 lakh pilgrims were recorded arriving here.
Badrinath Temple worships Badrinarayan, a form of the Hindu god Vishnu. It houses a 1 metre (3.3 ft) tall Shaligram idol believed to have been brought out from the nearby Narada Kunda in the 8th century by Adi Shankaracharya and installed there. The idol is considered by many Hindus as one of the eight Swayam Vyakta Kshetras (self-manifested idols) of Vishnu. Although the temple is located in North India, the chief priests, called "Raval" , are Nambudiri Brahmins from the southern Indian state of Kerala . Kedarnath Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand state of India . Kedarnath Temple is situated in the lap of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand and is one of the four Dhams [a] and Panch Kedar [b] along with being included in the twelve Jyotirlingas . Due to the adverse climate here, this temple is open for darshan only between April to November . It is said about this temple made of stones in the Katyuri style that it was built by Maharaja Janmejaya, the grandson of the Pandavas .
There is no historical evidence about the age of this temple, but Kedarnath has been an important pilgrimage for over a thousand years.
